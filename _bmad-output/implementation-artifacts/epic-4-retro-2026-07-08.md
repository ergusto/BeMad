## What went well

- **The measure-and-document discipline held.** 4-2/4-3/4-4 were verification stories with a strict "no app/lib behaviour change" fence — and it held. The one tempting app change (post-delete focus) was deliberately deferred rather than smuggled in.
- **Honest scoping over vanity metrics.** The coverage gate was scoped to the logic layer Vitest actually owns (`lib/**` + `app/api/**`), with the `.tsx` UI covered by E2E — and when review showed the reducer was wrongly excluded, we fixed the *scoping* (extracted the pure reducer) rather than gaming or hand-waving the number. Final: ~95% logic coverage, gate at 85/80.
- **Real evidence produced:** axe zero-critical WCAG 2.2 AA, XSS-inert rendering proven for two vectors with a real-DB round-trip, NFR-1 budgets measured (optimistic ~15–30 ms, reconcile p95 ~70 ms) — all in QA reports under `docs/qa/`.
- **CI now enforces the whole gate** (typecheck, lint, coverage gate, E2E) against a real Postgres — the quality bar is mechanical, not manual.

## What was hard / what we learned

- **Doc/reality drift is the dominant defect class of a verification epic.** Three of four stories had a review finding that was fundamentally "a document claims something the code/repo doesn't back": `all: true` that wasn't in the config, a reducer "counted" that wasn't, "24 stories", "39 tests". **Lesson:** when a doc cites a number, config key, or file, verify it against the source *at write time* — don't carry a figure forward from an earlier doc (the "39" came from the 4-2 coverage doc, written before 4-3 added the security spec).
- **Blanket rules over-reach silently.** `exclude: ["**/*.tsx"]` was a clean categorical boundary that also swept up a genuinely-unit-tested pure module because it happened to live in a `.tsx` file. **Lesson:** when applying a categorical include/exclude, enumerate what legitimate things it captures.
- **A green test can measure the wrong thing.** The perf "chromium-only" skip never fired (both Playwright projects use the chromium *engine*, not distinct `browserName`s), and a "p95" over 15 samples is arithmetically the max. **Lesson (recurring from the Epic-2 "guard against no-op-passing tests" item):** verify a test actually exercises/skips/measures its intended target — especially skip conditions and statistics.
- **Adversarial review is still the reliable correctness signal, not the implementer's confidence.** Every Epic 4 story shipped green suites that the review then improved. Passing tests ≠ correct.
